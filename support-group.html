Support group

OSC events offer moms a few hours to get out of their usual environments and be themselves. Join a supportive community and develop meaningful relationships with others through events that are specifically tailored for moms of children diagnosed with cancer and other chronic illnesses. Attending these events will not only ignite enjoyment for her but also for the family.

Questions? Contact us at programs@afreebird.org

According to Kazak A.E, Ph.D. out of all pediatric cancer survivor's families, 29.6% of mothers met the criteria for a PTSD diagnosis at some point since their child's diagnosis with cancer and 11.5% of fathers fulfilled the same criteria.

This is a striking statistic when compared to the reported rates of current PTSD for adults exposed to violent crimes or tragic deaths, which is around 7%.

What is support group?

Support group creates a comprehensive support system to enhance the lifestyle of caregivers. As a caregiver, it is easy to feel overwhelmed with the amount of information, appointments, and financial burden that comes along with caring for someone with a chronic disease. As physical and mental exhaustion sets in, it is easy to withdraw from friends and family.

A free bird recognizes the need for help and therefore seeks to provide support to the caregivers of children diagnosed with cancer by providing information, resources, and education regarding arts, sports, and nutrition. Past events that AFB support has held include healthy cooking classes providing caregivers with knowledge of the nutritional needs of children battling cancer.

Battling cancer is a tough job, so having a Support Group is a necessity.
